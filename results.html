Seller credibility layer

Seller experience is used after GLM, with shrinkage.

Seller ID is not directly modelled because the seller population is sparse. The credibility layer lets larger sellers carry more of their own experience while small sellers stay near their GLM risk mix.

Credibility formula Z = n / (n + 500)

Raw relativity is capped from 0.50 to 2.00, then normalized so total credibility expected loss preserves GLM expected loss.

Stress testing

Combined frequency and severity stress creates high pressure.

Stress testing turns the model output into a pricing control. The base portfolio is near target, but simultaneous frequency and severity deterioration moves the loss ratio materially above target.

GLM vs XGBoost challenger

XGBoost adds a slight ranking lift, not a better rate basis.

XGBoost is useful as a nonlinear challenger and diagnostic score. It is not selected as champion because the pricing decision depends on pure premium A/E and loss-ratio calibration.

Limitations and production data

The framework is transferable; the current rates are not production rates.

The result is a pricing architecture and governance story. Production deployment would require real platform experience and insurer-level assumptions.

Current limitation

Synthetic claims, Olist data rather than TikTok Shop data, and no true out-of-time validation.

Missing insurance layers

No claim development, IBNR, expense model, capital load, reinsurance, or compliance review.

Production input need

Real order, shipment, return, claim, payment, recovery, seller, and policy-period data.
